Our Mission

Our project is an examination of mental health services in Los Angeles County, given the current crises that impacts people struggling with mental health. We map where services currently exist and for whom â€” overlaying mental health facilities on zip codes filled with demographic information. This allows for thick analysis of mental health crisis and service provision, which is shaped by societal structures, including institutional racism and class inequality. Thus our map is both a resource for identifying services and a tool for researchers to analyze gaps in existing mental health services. We hope that this empowers people struggling with mental health and those fighting to expand mental health resources.

Why it matters?

Approximately 4% of adults in the U.S. suffer from serious mental conditions.* Many of these individuals suffer from their mental illness in silence, and many times without any diagnosis. Raising awareness about the importance of mental health is pivotal to ensure individuals receive the treatments needed and improve the quality of life of these same individuals.
